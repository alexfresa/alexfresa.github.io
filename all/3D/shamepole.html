Shame Pole

Sculpture created as an interpretation of a Northwest Coast shame pole that attempts to take its practice into a deeper emotional territory. The piece attempts to redefine "shame" and illustrate exile through color and material.

The structure of the piece includes a cement base underneath a plastic utility case that was later boxed in with wood. After the head piece was secured, it was later ornamented with found objects that were dyed black for a uniform appearance.
