Ain't Gonna Rain

Visual elements of the performance consist of white house paint and a full sheet of reflective mylar. The mylar faces the audience as a projection is cast upon it. As the mylar is painted, the projection slowly begins to appear. The final projection is unknown to the audience until the painting process is complete. Through the aftermath, it becomes clear that the projection depicts found footage of an apartment complex that has been engulfed in flames due to mishandled fireworks.

Conceptually, this performance offers a unique examination of time and demonstrates its unforgiving relationship with humankind through individual interactions. A feeling of disturbance amongst anticipation is built throughout its entirety.
